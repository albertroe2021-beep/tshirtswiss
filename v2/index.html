Custom Product Development

Custom Clothing Manufactured to Your Specifications

Professional apparel manufacturing starts with clear product specifications. TShirtSwiss helps define the garment type, fit, fabric weight, colour direction, branding method and finishing details before production begins. This gives Swiss businesses more control over the final product and reduces the uncertainty that often comes with international sourcing.

Our production support can include cotton t-shirts, polos, hoodies, sweatshirts, jackets, workwear, corporate uniforms, sportswear, rashguards, shorts, caps, bags and promotional merchandise. For clients developing a private label range, we can also assist with custom labels, neck labels, woven patches, hang tags, packaging and repeat production planning.

Decoration and branding options may include screen printing, embroidery, sublimation, heat transfer, custom trims and product-specific finishing. The correct method depends on the garment, fabric, design complexity, order quantity and intended use, which is why we review these details before confirming the most suitable production route.

Whether the requirement is a simple branded uniform order or a more detailed OEM clothing programme, the goal remains the same: to manufacture clothing that looks professional, feels reliable and can be delivered with clear communication from sample approval through to final shipment.

Our Production Process
Manufacturing Process

From Concept to Delivery

The TShirtSwiss process is built to make custom apparel manufacturing easier for Swiss businesses. It begins with an enquiry, where we review the type of clothing required, the intended use, branding requirements, quantities, preferred fabrics, sizing needs and any delivery deadlines that may affect the project.

Once the project direction is understood, we help prepare the production brief and quotation. This may include fabric recommendations, garment options, branding methods, sample requirements and estimated production timelines. For more detailed projects, artwork preparation and mockups can be used to confirm how the final product should look before sampling.

After sample approval, the order moves into production. During this stage, the factory follows the approved specification while the project is managed through production updates, quality checks, packing preparation and shipment coordination. This gives clients a more transparent view of what is happening without needing to manage the factory directly.

When production is complete, the order is checked, packed and prepared for international delivery. For businesses that require ongoing supply, we can also support repeat orders and long-term manufacturing programmes, helping maintain consistent products across future staff uniforms, teamwear, promotional campaigns or private label collections.
