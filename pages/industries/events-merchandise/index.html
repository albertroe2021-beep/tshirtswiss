Choosing Products, Decoration and Packaging for Merchandise

The best merchandise mix depends on audience, budget, deadline and brand purpose. T-shirts are versatile for events, fans and giveaways. Hoodies create a more premium product for retail, tours and communities. Caps and tote bags extend the collection and are practical for festivals, conferences and campaigns. Product selection should consider fabric feel, colour, fit, print area, packing requirements and whether the item is a giveaway, staff uniform or retail product.

Decoration should match the product and artwork. Screen printing is strong for bold graphics and larger quantities. Heat transfer can support detailed artwork, names and selected technical placements. Embroidery works well for caps, polos, jackets and premium marks. Labels, hang tags, size stickers, polybags and carton markings help merchandise feel professional and make distribution easier for event teams and ecommerce operations.

Planning Orders for Events, Tours and Campaigns

Merchandise orders often include many moving parts: artwork approval, garment selection, size breakdowns, packaging, deadlines, shipping and distribution. For events, timing is critical. For bands and creators, presentation and repeatability matter. For corporate campaigns, consistency and brand control are essential. Planning these details before production helps avoid delays and makes the final delivery easier to manage.

Orders can be packed by SKU, size, event location, staff group, retail bundle or campaign shipment. This reduces sorting time and helps teams move quickly when goods arrive. For repeat merchandise drops, approved products, colours, print positions, label details and packing rules can be documented so the next order follows the same specification.
