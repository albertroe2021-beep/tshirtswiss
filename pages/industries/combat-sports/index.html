Fabric Types and Benefits for Combat Sports Apparel

Combat sports garments need to handle movement, sweat, stretching, friction and repeated washing. Fabric should be selected based on activity, fit, artwork requirements and whether the product is used for training, competition, teamwear or merchandise.

Sublimation Polyester

Ideal for rashguards, fight shorts and full-colour designs that need durable artwork integrated into the fabric.

Polyester-Spandex Blends

Useful for compression garments, sports bras and rashguards requiring stretch and recovery.

Satin or Gloss Polyester

Common direction for Muay Thai shorts where shine, movement and bold colour presentation are desired.

Dry-Fit Polyester

Good for team shirts, training tops and gym apparel in hot climates or high-sweat environments.

Cotton Jersey

Suitable for casual gym shirts, fan merchandise and lifestyle products.

Fleece and French Terry

Best for hoodies, warm-up apparel and premium gym merchandise.

Training and Brand Suitability

Fightwear Should Support Movement, Durability and Identity

Combat sports apparel is highly visible and heavily used. It needs to support movement while representing the fighter, gym or brand. From a practical training perspective, garments should consider stretch, seam comfort, heat comfort, durability, artwork placement and wash performance.

  • Stretch fabrics support grappling, striking, clinch work and gym movement.
  • Sublimation helps maintain full-colour artwork on performance garments.
  • Breathable dry-fit fabrics are useful for hot gyms and tropical climates.
  • Reinforced construction matters for fight shorts and high-movement garments.
  • Team colours and sponsor placement help gyms and fighters look professional.
  • Private label finishing helps fightwear brands sell online and internationally.
