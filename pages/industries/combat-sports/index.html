Fabric Types and Benefits for Combat Sports Apparel

Combat sports garments need to handle movement, sweat, stretching, friction and repeated washing. Fabric should be selected based on activity, fit, artwork requirements and whether the product is used for training, teamwear or merchandise.

Sublimation Polyester

Ideal for rashguards, sport shorts and full-colour designs that need durable artwork integrated into the fabric.

Polyester-Spandex Blends

Useful for compression-style garments and rashguards requiring stretch and recovery.

Satin or Gloss Polyester

Common direction for Muay Thai shorts where shine, movement and bold colour presentation are desired.

Dry-Tech Polyester

Good for team shirts, training tops and gym apparel in hot climates or high-sweat environments.

Cotton Jersey

Suitable for casual gym shirts, fan merchandise and lifestyle products.

Fleece and French Terry

Best for hoodies, warm-up apparel and premium gym merchandise.

Training and Brand Suitability

Performance Apparel Should Support Movement, Durability and Identity

Combat sports apparel is highly visible and heavily used. It needs to support movement while representing the gym, team or brand. From a practical training perspective, garments should consider stretch, seam comfort, heat comfort, durability, artwork placement and wash performance.

  • Stretch fabrics support high-movement training and close-fitting garments.
  • Sublimation helps maintain full-colour artwork on polyester performance garments.
  • Breathable dry-tech fabrics are useful for hot gyms and tropical climates.
  • Stronger construction matters for shorts and high-movement garments.
  • Team colours and sponsor placement help gyms and teams look professional.
  • Private label finishing helps sportswear brands sell online and internationally.
