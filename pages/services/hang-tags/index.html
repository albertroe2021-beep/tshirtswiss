Hang Tags Help Customers Understand the Product

A hang tag can communicate brand story, product features, sizing, pricing, barcode information, care notes or collection identity. For private label and retail apparel, hang tags help the garment feel more complete and professionally presented.

Hang tag planning should be coordinated with labels, packaging and product specifications so the final order is easy to sell, store, distribute or ship.

What Hang Tags Can Include

Hang tags can be simple or detailed depending on how the garment will be sold or distributed. The right information depends on the product, market and fulfilment method.

Brand Story

Short brand messaging, collection identity, launch theme or product positioning.

Product Details

Fabric information, fit notes, style name, collection name or product benefits.

Pricing and Barcodes

Useful for retail, wholesale, boutiques, distributors and ecommerce inventory workflows.

Care Information

Short care notes or references that support the garment care label.

Size and SKU Details

Helpful for product organisation, stock control, fulfilment partners and retail handling.

QR Codes or Links

Can direct customers to product pages, brand stories, sizing guides or care pages where appropriate.

Tag Planning

Plan Hang Tags With Labels and Packaging

Hang tags work best when they are part of the full product presentation. Labels, folding, stickers, polybags and carton organisation should all support the same brand and distribution goal.

  • Keep hang tag artwork consistent with garment labels and packaging.
  • Confirm whether tags need pricing, barcode, SKU or size information.
  • Choose tag size around the garment type and retail presentation.
  • Consider string, pin or attachment method early in production planning.
  • Coordinate tags with polybags, stickers and carton labels for ecommerce.
  • Review hang tags during sampling if retail presentation is important.

Hang Tag Production Workflow

Hang tags should be planned before final production so artwork, information fields and attachment details are approved on time.

1. Brand and Product Review

We review the garment type, brand style, product category and target use.

2. Information Planning

We confirm text, pricing, SKU, barcode, care notes, QR codes or collection messaging.

3. Artwork Preparation

Artwork is prepared for the chosen tag size, layout and production requirements.

4. Sample or Approval

Tags can be reviewed during sampling or before bulk production where needed.

5. Attachment and Packing

Tags are attached or packed according to the approved garment and packaging plan.

6. Final Check

Finished products are checked for tag placement, presentation and packing readiness.
