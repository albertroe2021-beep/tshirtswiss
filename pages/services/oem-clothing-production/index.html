A More Controlled Way to Manufacture

From Sample, Spec or Idea to Repeatable Apparel Production

OEM production is where design intent becomes a physical product. It requires more than sewing capacity. It requires decisions about fabric behaviour, shrinkage, measurements, decoration, labels, carton packing, size ratios and inspection points. The earlier these details are clarified, the more stable the final production run becomes.

Built for Professional Buyers

OEM Manufacturing Gives You Control Over the Product

Choosing a standard blank garment can work for simple merchandise, but it limits your ability to control fit, fabric, colour, construction, trims, labels and packing. OEM manufacturing gives your business more ownership over the final product. This is important when garments must match a brand identity, meet team uniform needs, support repeat orders or sit within a retail product range.

We help review your requirements and identify what needs to be confirmed before production. If a tech pack is available, we can work from it. If you only have a sample or reference product, we can help translate that into a clearer production direction. When the product requires development, we can connect OEM production with product development and sampling so the bulk order is based on an approved version.

  • Fabric and trim planning
  • Screen print, embroidery and sublimation
  • Labels, hang tags and packaging
  • Bulk production coordination
  • Quality control and shipping support

OEM vs Blank Apparel

When OEM Production Makes More Sense

Blank garments are useful for fast, simple orders. OEM production is stronger when the product itself needs to be more controlled, more differentiated or more repeatable.

Fabric Direction

Material Decisions Shape the Final Garment

Fabric is one of the most important OEM decisions. It affects comfort, fit, shrinkage, print result, durability, cost and how the garment feels in daily use. We help customers choose material directions that match the product goal rather than selecting fabric in isolation.

Cotton & Cotton Blends

Performance Fabrics

Workwear Materials

Chosen for durability, repeated washing, team presentation and practical day-to-day use.

Branding and Finishing

More Than Decoration on a Finished Garment

For OEM orders, branding can be built into the garment rather than added at the end. That may include screen printing, embroidery, sublimation, heat transfer, custom neck labels, woven labels, care labels, hang tags, packaging, SKU stickers and carton organisation. The best approach depends on the garment, artwork, fabric and intended use.

A corporate polo might need subtle embroidery and consistent sizing across a staff group. A sportswear product might require sublimation so artwork becomes part of the fabric. A private label garment might require custom labels and packaging so it arrives ready for ecommerce fulfilment or retail presentation. These choices should be made before sampling so the sample reflects the final product as closely as possible.

  • Screen printing for bold graphics
  • Embroidery for premium logos
  • Sublimation for all-over sportswear artwork
  • Heat transfer for selected finishes
  • Custom labels and care labels
  • Hang tags, bags and carton rules

OEM FAQs

Common Questions About OEM Clothing Production

What does OEM clothing production mean?

OEM means garments are produced according to your specifications, samples or technical requirements rather than simply buying a standard blank item.

Do I need a tech pack?

A tech pack is helpful, but not always required. We can also work from samples, reference garments, size charts and detailed written requirements.

Can you help with sampling?

Yes. Sampling is recommended when fit, fabric, labels, decoration or construction details need to be confirmed before bulk production.

Can you produce private label clothing?

Yes. OEM production can include private label elements such as custom neck labels, woven labels, hang tags, packaging and retail presentation.

Which products can be made through OEM production?

We support t-shirts, polos, hoodies, jackets, uniforms, workwear, sportswear, rashguards, shorts, caps, bags and private label apparel ranges.

Can you ship to Switzerland?

Yes. Orders can be shipped to Switzerland, Europe and other international destinations depending on the production and delivery requirements.
