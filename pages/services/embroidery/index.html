Embroidery Gives Apparel a Professional, Durable Finish

Embroidery is often the best branding method when the garment needs to look premium and last through repeated wear. It is especially useful for corporate polos, caps, jackets, hospitality uniforms, healthcare apparel and workwear where subtle professional branding matters.

The best embroidery result depends on the garment fabric, logo detail, stitch count, placement and backing. Artwork that works well on screen may need to be simplified for embroidery so it remains readable and clean.

Embroidery Planning

Good Embroidery Starts With the Right Artwork and Placement

Embroidery is not simply printing with thread. Stitching has physical thickness, so very small text, gradients, thin lines and complex artwork may need adjustment. Placement also affects comfort and appearance.

  • Simple logos and strong shapes usually embroider better than complex artwork.
  • Small text should be checked for readability before production.
  • Left chest, cap front, sleeve, back neck and hem placements each have different limits.
  • Thread colour should be selected against garment colour and brand guidelines.
  • Stable fabrics usually give cleaner embroidery than very thin or high-stretch fabrics.
  • Sampling is useful when logo detail, placement or garment fabric needs approval.

Embroidery Production Workflow

Embroidery should be planned as part of the production process so artwork, stitch direction and garment suitability are approved before bulk manufacturing.

1. Artwork Review

We review your logo, colours, size and intended garment type.

2. Placement Planning

We confirm whether the logo should be on the chest, cap front, sleeve, back or another location.

3. Digitising

Artwork is prepared for embroidery so the machine can stitch it correctly.

4. Sample or Approval

Where needed, embroidery can be reviewed during sampling or pre-production approval.

5. Bulk Embroidery

Embroidery is applied according to the approved placement and specification.

6. Final Check

Finished garments are reviewed for logo placement, thread quality and presentation.
