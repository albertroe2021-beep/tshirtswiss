Embroidery Advantage

A Premium Branding Method for Professional Apparel

Embroidery gives apparel a stitched, textured and long-lasting finish that feels more premium than many printed decoration methods. It is especially popular for polo shirts, caps, jackets, softshells, hospitality uniforms, corporate clothing, workwear, bags and private label garments where a clean logo needs to look professional over repeated use.

For Swiss companies, clubs and brands, embroidery is often chosen when the garment must represent the organisation in a polished way. A small chest logo on a polo shirt, a cap front logo, a sleeve logo on a jacket or a subtle tone-on-tone mark can make apparel feel more permanent and considered than a temporary promotional item.

TShirtSwiss.ch helps buyers plan embroidery as part of the complete apparel production workflow. We consider the logo artwork, garment fabric, stitch density, size, placement, thread colours, sampling requirements and quality control before bulk production begins. This helps prevent common issues such as logos that are too small, overly detailed artwork, puckering on unsuitable fabric, inconsistent placement or thread colours that do not match the brand direction.

Artwork Becomes Stitching

Strong Embroidery Starts with the Right Logo and Garment Choice

Embroidery is not simply printing with thread. The artwork must be digitised, simplified where needed and matched to a suitable garment. Stitch direction, thread colour, garment weight, backing, placement and logo size all influence the final appearance.

Embroidery Planning

What Should Be Confirmed Before Embroidery?

Before embroidery begins, the logo needs to be reviewed for stitch suitability. Thin lines, tiny text, gradients, detailed illustrations and complex shading may not translate well into thread. The final result depends on how the artwork is digitised and how it interacts with the chosen fabric.

Garment choice also matters. Polos, caps, jackets and structured apparel usually work very well with embroidery. Lightweight stretch fabrics, very thin garments or highly technical materials may need extra planning or another decoration method. We help customers choose a decoration route that fits both the logo and the garment.

  • Logo review and embroidery digitising
  • Thread colour and brand matching
  • Logo size and placement direction
  • Garment type, fabric and thickness
  • Sampling or stitch proof where required
  • Quality checks before packing

Logo Digitising and Stitch Planning

The Embroidery File Is as Important as the Original Logo

A logo designed for a website or business card is not automatically ready for embroidery. It needs to be translated into stitches. Digitising decides stitch direction, density, sequence, thread changes and how the logo will behave on the garment. Good digitising helps the embroidery look cleaner and reduces issues during production.

Small text, thin lines and fine gradients may need to be adjusted. Sometimes the best result comes from simplifying the logo or choosing a slightly larger placement. A professional embroidery plan respects both the brand and the physical limits of stitching.

  • Digitising for stitch production
  • Thread colour planning
  • Logo size recommendations
  • Placement review by garment type
  • Proofing for important projects
  • Quality checks before packing

Embroidery FAQs

Common Questions About Custom Embroidery

What garments are best for embroidery?

Embroidery works especially well on polos, caps, jackets, softshells, uniforms, hospitality apparel, workwear and structured garments.

Does my logo need to be digitised?

Yes. Artwork must be converted into a stitch file before embroidery production. This controls stitch direction, density and thread changes.

Can embroidery handle small text?

Sometimes, but very small text may lose clarity. We recommend reviewing logo size and detail before confirming embroidery.

Can embroidery be used for private label apparel?

Yes. Embroidery can be combined with custom labels, hang tags, packaging and OEM manufacturing for private label garments.

Is embroidery better than screen printing?

It depends on the garment and artwork. Embroidery is often better for premium logos, while screen printing is better for large graphics.

Can I approve a sample first?

Yes. For important orders, sampling or stitch proofing can help confirm size, placement and thread colours before bulk production.
