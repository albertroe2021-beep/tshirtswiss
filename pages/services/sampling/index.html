Sampling Reduces Risk and Improves Production Accuracy

A sample gives you a physical reference for what will be produced. It helps confirm whether the garment matches the intended specification before production begins, and it creates a clear approval point for the manufacturing team.

For brands, sampling protects customer experience. For companies ordering uniforms, it helps confirm comfort and presentation. For OEM buyers, it creates a production reference that can be checked against bulk manufacturing.

Our Apparel Sampling Process

We manage sampling with clear approval points so you understand what is being tested and what needs feedback before production.

1. Sample Brief

We review the garment type, artwork, reference sample, fabric request, sizing and purpose.

2. Specification Confirmation

We clarify fabric, measurements, decoration, labels, packaging and construction details.

3. Sample Production

The sample is produced according to the agreed instructions and available materials.

4. Review and Feedback

You review fit, comfort, appearance, branding, finishing and any required changes.

5. Revision if Needed

If important changes are required, the specification can be adjusted before production.

6. Bulk Approval

Once approved, the sample becomes the production reference for the bulk order.

What Samples Help Confirm

Fit, Fabric, Branding and Finishing

Each sample should answer practical questions before production begins. Does the fabric feel right? Does the garment fit the intended wearer? Is the logo size balanced? Are the labels and packaging suitable for the brand?

  • Measurements, length, width, sleeve shape and shoulder balance.
  • Fabric weight, stretch, texture, breathability and opacity.
  • Print, embroidery, sublimation, heat transfer or label positioning.
  • Care labels, hang tags, neck prints, folding and packaging.
  • Comfort, movement and suitability for the intended role or activity.
  • Details that should be documented before bulk production.
