Why It Matters

Quality Control Protects Your Product, Timeline and Brand

Apparel production involves many details: fabric quality, cutting, stitching, measurements, print placement, embroidery, labels, finishing, folding, packing and shipping. If these details are not controlled, small issues can become expensive problems once the order reaches the client or customer.

Our quality control process helps reduce risk by setting clear expectations before production and checking the order against those expectations throughout the project. This is especially important for private label brands, corporate uniform programmes, healthcare apparel, sportswear and repeat production orders.

Process

Our Apparel Quality Control Process

We structure quality control around clear approval points and practical production checks.

1

Specification Review

We check product details, artwork, fabric, size chart, label requirements, packaging and delivery expectations.

2

Sample Approval

The approved sample becomes the reference for fit, fabric, construction, decoration and finishing.

3

Material and Trim Check

Fabric, labels, hang tags, trims and packaging are reviewed against the confirmed order details.

4

In-Process Inspection

Production is checked for measurements, stitching, construction, print or embroidery placement and consistency.

5

Final Inspection

Finished garments are reviewed for defects, sizing, branding, finishing, folding and presentation.

6

Packing Verification

We check packing format, size stickers, carton labels, quantities and shipment readiness.

Inspection Areas

What We Check Before Garments Ship

Quality control must be matched to the product type. A polo shirt needs collar and placket checks. A private label order needs label and packaging verification. Sportswear needs fabric and decoration compatibility. Uniform programmes need sizing and colour consistency across repeat orders.

Fabric and Construction

Fabric suitability, seams, stitching, hems, collars, cuffs, plackets, pockets and overall finish.

Measurements and Fit

Size accuracy, garment balance, length, width, sleeve measurements and tolerance control.

Branding and Decoration

Print placement, embroidery quality, sublimation alignment, heat transfer and logo accuracy.

Labels and Packaging

Woven labels, care labels, neck prints, hang tags, size stickers, polybags and carton preparation.
