Quality Control Advantage

Good Manufacturing Needs Clear Inspection Points

Quality control is one of the most important parts of apparel manufacturing because small issues can become expensive problems after the order has shipped. A garment may look acceptable at first glance but still have sizing inconsistencies, stitching issues, label mistakes, colour variation, print placement problems, embroidery defects or packing errors.

For Swiss businesses, private label brands, ecommerce stores, sports teams, corporate uniform buyers and promotional clients, quality control helps protect the final customer experience. It gives the buyer more confidence that the order has been reviewed against the approved sample, product specification and practical delivery requirements before it leaves the production environment.

TShirtSwiss.ch treats quality control as part of the full manufacturing workflow rather than a final afterthought. From product development and sampling through OEM production, private label finishing and worldwide shipping, quality expectations should be defined early and checked at the correct points. This creates a more reliable path from approved sample to finished order.

QC Protects the Brand Experience

The Final Garment Must Match What the Buyer Approved

An approved sample is only useful if the bulk order is checked against it. Quality control helps confirm that the production run reflects the intended product: the right fabric, right sizing, right decoration, right labels, right packing and right presentation for the end customer.

Inspection Details

What Should Be Checked Before Apparel Ships?

Quality control is more than looking for obvious defects. The inspection should consider the approved sample, technical requirements, buyer expectations and final use of the garment. A corporate uniform order may need consistent sizing and logo placement across departments. A private label collection may need accurate labels, hang tags and packaging. A sportswear order may require stretch, stitching strength and decoration durability.

Each order has different risk points. We help focus the review on the details that matter for the specific product: measurements, stitching, seams, colour, fabric hand feel, print result, embroidery, sublimation, labels, trims, folding, carton packing and SKU organisation.

  • Measurements and size tolerance
  • Stitching, seams and construction
  • Fabric quality and colour consistency
  • Print, embroidery and decoration placement
  • Labels, hang tags and packaging
  • Carton organisation and shipment readiness

QC Focus Areas

What We Look For During Apparel Quality Control

A useful quality control process is structured. It checks the garment as a product, not only as a piece of fabric. The inspection should ask whether the order is wearable, brand-correct, packed properly and ready to reach the customer.

1

Measurements

Check size points, garment length, width, sleeve dimensions and consistency against the approved direction.

2

Construction

Review seams, stitching, loose threads, hems, trims and general manufacturing finish.

3

Branding

Inspect screen printing, embroidery, sublimation, label position, logo placement and artwork accuracy.

4

Packing

Confirm folding, polybags, hang tags, SKU stickers, size ratios and carton organisation.

Packing and Shipment Readiness

Quality Control Should Include the Way the Order Is Organised

For many apparel orders, the garment itself is only one part of the final delivery. The order also needs to be packed in a way that supports the buyer's workflow. A sports club may need garments separated by team, size or player. A corporate buyer may need cartons organised by department or branch. A private label brand may need each item packed with size stickers and retail tags.

Checking packing details before shipment helps avoid confusion after delivery. It can reduce sorting time, prevent fulfilment mistakes and improve the customer experience. For ecommerce and private label orders, packing consistency can be just as important as garment consistency because it affects how the product is received.

  • Folding and individual packing
  • Size and colour separation
  • SKU stickers and carton marks
  • Hang tags and retail presentation
  • Branch, team or department packing
  • Shipment readiness before dispatch

Quality Control FAQs

Common Questions About Apparel QC

What does apparel quality control check?

Quality control can check measurements, stitching, seams, fabric, colour, decoration, labels, hang tags, packaging and carton organisation.

Is QC needed if a sample was approved?

Yes. The approved sample is the reference, but the bulk order still needs to be checked for consistency before shipment.

Can QC include private label details?

Yes. Private label QC can include neck labels, woven labels, care labels, hang tags, packaging, SKU stickers and retail presentation.

Can issues be corrected before shipping?

Where possible, issues found before dispatch can be reviewed and corrected before the order is released for shipment.

Which products can be inspected?

T-shirts, polos, hoodies, uniforms, workwear, sportswear, rashguards, shorts, caps, bags and private label apparel can be reviewed.

Does QC replace good production planning?

No. QC supports production planning. The best results come from clear specifications, approved samples and structured checks.
