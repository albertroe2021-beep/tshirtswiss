Full-Colour Sportswear

Vibrant Prints Built Into Performance Apparel

Sublimation printing is a strong decoration method for polyester sportswear and performance apparel. Instead of sitting heavily on top of the garment, sublimation becomes part of the fabric surface, making it suitable for lightweight, breathable garments used in training, events and competition.

TShirtSwiss.ch supports sublimation printing through Swiss-managed apparel production in Thailand. We help with fabric suitability, artwork setup, panel planning, sampling, production and quality control for rashguards, MMA shorts, Muay Thai shorts, jerseys and teamwear.

Sublimation Printing for Rashguards, MMA Shorts, Muay Thai Shorts and Teamwear

Sublimation printing is especially useful when apparel needs full-colour graphics, all-over artwork, sponsor logos, team names, gradients, patterns or complex visual designs. It is widely used for combat sports, fitness apparel, running tops, cycling jerseys, tournament apparel and teamwear.

The method works best on suitable polyester or polyester-rich fabrics. Fabric colour, stretch, garment pattern, seam placement and artwork layout all affect the final result. Planning these details early helps prevent misaligned graphics, poor colour results, distortion or artwork being lost in seam areas.

Why Sublimation Works Well for Combat Sports and Teamwear

Combat sports and performance apparel often require strong graphics without heavy decoration that cracks or adds weight. Sublimation makes it possible to create colourful sponsor layouts, club graphics, patterns and team identities while keeping the garment suitable for movement.

For brands and clubs, sublimation also makes repeat production easier once the artwork, garment pattern and fabric are approved. This is useful for team kits, fightwear ranges, tournament drops, gym apparel and private label sportswear products.

FAQ

Sublimation Printing Questions

What is sublimation printing best for?

Sublimation is best for full-colour sportswear, performance apparel, rashguards, fight shorts, jerseys and polyester garments.

Can sublimation be used on cotton?

No. Sublimation works best on suitable polyester or polyester-rich fabrics, not standard cotton garments.

Can you print all-over designs?

Yes. Sublimation is well suited to all-over artwork when the garment pattern and panel layout are planned correctly.

Is sublimation good for combat sports?

Yes. It is commonly used for rashguards, MMA shorts, Muay Thai shorts and other performance garments.

Can I review a sample first?

Yes. Sampling is recommended to review colour, print placement, fit and garment finish before bulk production.

Can sublimation designs be reordered?

Yes. Approved artwork, fabric and garment specifications can be documented for repeat production.
