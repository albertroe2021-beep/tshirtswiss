Sublimation Is Built for Colourful Performance Apparel

Sublimation printing is useful when artwork needs to cover large areas, include many colours or become part of the fabric rather than sitting heavily on top. It is commonly used for teamwear, rashguards, MMA shorts, Muay Thai shorts, cycling-style apparel and other polyester-based sportswear.

The method depends heavily on fabric suitability. Sublimation is generally planned around polyester or polyester-rich fabrics, and it is most effective when colour, artwork placement and panel layout are considered during product development.

When Sublimation Printing Works Best

Sublimation is best selected when artwork, fabric and garment construction are planned together. It can be a strong option for performance apparel where colour, durability and movement matter.

All-Over Designs

Useful for garments with full-colour artwork across panels, sleeves, shorts or large garment areas.

Polyester Sportswear

Works best on suitable polyester or polyester-rich fabrics used in activewear and teamwear.

Team and Sponsor Graphics

Good for logos, names, club graphics and sponsor layouts that need multiple colours.

Technical Apparel

Useful for rashguards, fightwear, training shirts and selected performance garments.

Private Label Sportswear

Can support distinctive brand-owned designs and repeat collection specifications.

Lightweight Apparel

Because the print becomes part of the fabric, sublimation can feel lighter than some surface prints.

Artwork and Fabric Planning

Sublimation Requires Artwork, Panels and Fabric to Work Together

Sublimation is not just a decoration added at the end. For many garments, the artwork should be planned around the pattern pieces, seams, panels and fabric behaviour.

  • Artwork should be prepared at the correct scale and resolution.
  • Panel layout matters for rashguards, shorts and technical garments.
  • Polyester or polyester-rich fabrics are usually required for strong sublimation results.
  • Seams and stretch areas can affect artwork alignment.
  • Colour expectations should be reviewed before production where possible.
  • Sampling is useful for checking fabric, fit, colour, artwork placement and construction.

Sublimation Production Workflow

Sublimation should be built into the production plan so artwork, fabric, pattern pieces and garment construction are aligned before bulk production.

1. Product Review

We review garment type, activity, artwork requirements, quantity and intended use.

2. Fabric Suitability

We confirm whether the fabric direction is suitable for sublimation and garment performance.

3. Artwork Planning

Artwork is prepared around panels, seams, print areas, logos and size requirements.

4. Sampling or Approval

Samples or approvals help check colour, fit, placement and construction before bulk production.

5. Bulk Printing and Sewing

Panels are printed and garments are produced according to the approved specification.

6. Final Inspection

Finished garments are checked for colour, alignment, stitching, labels and packing readiness.
