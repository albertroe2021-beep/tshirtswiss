Screen Printing Is Ideal for Strong Graphic Apparel

Screen printing is a popular decoration method for apparel because it produces bold, clear artwork and works well for repeat designs across larger production runs. It is especially useful for t-shirts, hoodies, event merchandise, gym apparel, corporate campaigns and promotional garments.

The best result depends on artwork complexity, garment colour, fabric type, print size, ink selection and order quantity. We help clients choose whether screen printing, embroidery, heat transfer or sublimation is the most suitable method.

Artwork Planning

Good Screen Printing Starts With Clear Artwork

Artwork should be prepared with the garment and print method in mind. Print size, colour count, detail level and fabric colour all influence the final result.

  • Vector artwork is preferred where possible.
  • Clear colour separation helps production planning.
  • Very fine details may need adjustment for consistent printing.
  • Large prints should be checked against garment size and placement.
  • Dark garments may require additional print planning for opacity.
  • Sampling or artwork approval is useful for important launches and retail products.

Screen Printing Production Workflow

Screen printing should be planned before production so artwork, garment choice, print placement and quality expectations are clear.

1. Artwork Review

We review logo files, design colours, print size and garment colour.

2. Garment Selection

We confirm whether the fabric and garment construction are suitable for screen printing.

3. Placement Planning

Prints are positioned for front, back, chest, sleeve or other garment locations.

4. Print Preparation

Artwork and colour requirements are prepared for production.

5. Bulk Printing

Garments are printed according to the confirmed artwork and placement specification.

6. Final Inspection

Finished garments are checked for print placement, appearance, finishing and packing readiness.
