Screen Printing Advantage

One of the Most Reliable Methods for Bold Apparel Graphics

Screen printing remains one of the most popular decoration methods for custom apparel because it delivers strong colour, good durability and a professional finish on suitable garments. It is especially useful for t-shirts, hoodies, sweatshirts, event merchandise, uniforms, gym apparel, club clothing and private label ranges where the artwork needs to look clean and consistent across a bulk order.

For Swiss brands and organisations, the challenge is not only choosing screen printing. The important part is making sure the artwork, garment fabric, print size, colour count and order quantity make sense together. A simple one-colour logo print on a cotton t-shirt has different requirements than a large multi-colour back print on hoodies or merchandise sold through an ecommerce store.

TShirtSwiss.ch helps buyers plan screen printing as part of the wider apparel manufacturing workflow. We review the garment type, artwork style, intended use, fabric direction and order requirements before production begins. This helps reduce print issues, improve consistency and make sure the finished garment feels appropriate for the customer, team, event or brand.

Artwork Meets Production

Great Screen Printing Starts Before the Ink Touches the Garment

Print quality depends on artwork preparation, garment choice, colour planning, print size, placement and production control. A strong design can still print poorly if the file is unsuitable, the fabric is wrong, the colour count is unrealistic or the placement is unclear. Planning these details early creates a stronger production result.

Print Planning

What Should Be Confirmed Before Screen Printing?

Before printing begins, the project needs a clear artwork and placement direction. This includes logo size, print position, number of print colours, garment colour, fabric type, quantity, packaging requirements and whether the order requires a sample or proof before bulk production.

Screen printing is strongest when the artwork is bold, clean and production friendly. It can work very well for logos, chest prints, back prints, event graphics, team graphics and brand merchandise. Very complex gradients, photographic images or highly detailed artwork may require another method depending on the garment and intended result.

  • Vector artwork or high-quality print file
  • Print size and placement direction
  • Garment type, fabric and colour
  • Number of print colours
  • Sampling or print proof where required
  • Packing and shipment requirements

Screen Printing vs Other Decoration

Choosing the Right Branding Method

Screen printing is excellent for many projects, but it is not the only option. The best method depends on garment type, artwork, quantity, texture and the look you want to achieve.

Artwork and Colour Planning

Print Results Depend on the Details Buyers Provide

The more clearly the artwork is prepared, the smoother the print production becomes. Print-ready vector files are usually preferred for logos and clean graphics. Buyers should also confirm the size of the print, placement on the garment, colour references and whether the artwork must match brand guidelines.

Garment colour also matters. A design that looks strong on a white t-shirt may need different treatment on a black hoodie or coloured polo. Ink colours, underbase requirements, fabric texture and print coverage all affect the final appearance. Planning these details early reduces surprises and improves consistency across the order.

  • Vector artwork preferred for logos
  • Clear print size and placement notes
  • Brand colours or Pantone references where possible
  • Garment colour and fabric compatibility
  • Sample or proof for important projects
  • Quality checks before packing

Screen Printing FAQs

Common Questions About Screen Printing

What garments are best for screen printing?

Screen printing works well on many t-shirts, hoodies, sweatshirts, uniforms and cotton or blend garments, depending on fabric and artwork.

Is screen printing good for bulk orders?

Yes. Screen printing is often a strong option for bulk orders because the setup can be used across a larger production run.

What artwork files are best?

Vector artwork is usually preferred for logos and clean graphics. High-quality artwork helps improve print clarity and production consistency.

Can screen printing be used for private label clothing?

Yes. Screen printing can be combined with custom labels, hang tags, packaging and OEM manufacturing for private label apparel.

Can I print on dark garments?

Yes, but dark garments may need additional planning such as underbase printing, colour review and sample approval for best results.

Is screen printing always the best method?

No. Embroidery, sublimation or heat transfer may be better depending on fabric, artwork complexity, quantity and desired finish.
