Manufacturing Support for Apparel Brands

A private label clothing project may begin with a sketch, a reference garment, a tech pack, a previous sample or a clear commercial idea. We help convert that starting point into a production-ready specification covering fabric, fit, sizing, construction, decoration, labels, packaging and delivery.

Private label manufacturing is about creating apparel that feels fully owned by your brand. The garment, fit, fabric, label, packaging and final presentation must work together for retail, ecommerce, wholesale or brand merchandise.

Our Private Label Manufacturing Process

We manage each step with structured communication so you know what is being developed, approved, produced and shipped.

1. Brand and Product Brief

We review your target customer, garment idea, product category, reference samples and brand requirements.

2. Specification Planning

We define fabric, fit, sizing, trims, labels, artwork, packaging and production details.

3. Sampling

Samples allow you to approve the garment before committing to bulk production.

4. Labels and Packaging

We coordinate woven labels, printed neck labels, hang tags, care labels, stickers and packing.

5. Bulk Manufacturing

Garments are produced according to the approved sample and confirmed specification.

6. Quality and Delivery

Finished goods are inspected, packed and prepared for international shipping.

Brand Details

Labels, Hang Tags and Packaging

The difference between a generic garment and a private label product is often in the details. Labels, tags and packaging communicate that the garment belongs to your brand and has been prepared for your customer experience.

  • Woven labels, printed neck labels, care labels and size labels.
  • Hang tags for product information, brand story, pricing or collection presentation.
  • Folded packing, polybags, stickers, barcode planning and carton labels.
  • Brand details aligned across t-shirts, hoodies, polos, sportswear, caps and accessories.
  • Sample approval for labels, tag placement, packaging and retail presentation.
  • Specification documentation for product drops and repeat production.
