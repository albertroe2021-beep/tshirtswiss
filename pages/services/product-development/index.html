Development Support

Build Better Apparel Before Production Begins

Product development is the stage where ideas become practical garments. It helps define the product purpose, target customer, fabric direction, fit, construction, decoration, labels, packaging and production requirements before bulk manufacturing begins.

TShirtSwiss.ch supports brands, companies and ecommerce labels with apparel product development through Swiss-managed coordination and experienced manufacturing partners in Thailand. We help identify what information is missing, what needs to be tested, and how to move from concept to sample approval.

Product Development for Brands, Retailers and Corporate Apparel Programmes

A successful garment starts with clear development work. This can include reviewing a reference garment, choosing a fabric, adjusting fit, planning logo placement, defining measurements, selecting trims, creating labels, and preparing packing rules. Without these details, production can become inconsistent or difficult to repeat.

Our role is to help make your apparel idea factory-ready. We work with the information you already have, whether that is a sketch, sample, mood board, tech pack, product photo or existing garment. From there, we help create a clearer path toward sampling, approval and production.

Technical Planning

From Rough Idea to Clear Garment Specification

Development decisions influence the final product more than any later production stage. Fabric choice, measurement tolerances, artwork placement, stitch type, label position and packing instructions all need to be practical and repeatable.

Development Inputs

  • Sketches, references and product photos
  • Physical samples or existing garments
  • Fabric direction and colour targets
  • Artwork, labels and packaging ideas

Development Outputs

  • Clear garment specifications
  • Sample and revision guidance
  • Production-ready details
  • Reorder-ready documentation

Why Product Development Matters Before Manufacturing

Good product development reduces risk. It gives you the opportunity to confirm fabric, fit, branding and construction before producing a larger order. It also helps prevent misunderstandings between the brand and the factory, because key details are documented before the production stage.

For ecommerce brands and private label collections, development also supports product pages, photography, packaging and restocks. A properly developed product can become a repeatable SKU, while a poorly defined product may be difficult to reproduce accurately later.

  • Fit and measurement review
  • Fabric and hand-feel validation
  • Artwork and logo placement checks

Creating a Foundation for Repeatable Production

Once a garment is developed and approved, the specifications can be reused for future production runs. This is especially important for brands that plan seasonal drops, restocks, team programmes or long-term uniform supply.

Approved development details can include fabric, colour, GSM, measurement chart, stitch details, artwork placement, label rules, packaging instructions and carton labelling. This documentation helps reduce variation and makes scaling easier.
