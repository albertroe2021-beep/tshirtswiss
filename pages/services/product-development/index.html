Better Garments Begin With Clear Specifications

Many apparel production problems happen because the product was not fully defined before sampling or bulk production. Fabric, fit, measurements, stitching, decoration, labels and packaging all influence cost, quality and delivery. Product development helps organise these details early.

We work with clients who have different starting points. Some have a tech pack. Some have a sample they want to improve. Some have a product idea or a category in mind. Our role is to help convert that information into a clear, practical path toward sampling and production.

Our Apparel Product Development Process

We structure development so the garment becomes clearer at each stage and can move into sampling with fewer unknowns.

1. Project Brief

We review your product idea, reference garment, target customer, quantity and commercial goals.

2. Product Direction

We define garment type, fabric direction, fit, sizing, construction and intended use.

3. Material Planning

We discuss fabric composition, GSM, stretch, texture, durability and comfort expectations.

4. Branding and Finishing

We plan print, embroidery, labels, hang tags, packaging and other brand details.

5. Sampling Instructions

The agreed details are prepared for sample development and approval.

6. Production Readiness

After sampling, the product can be refined for bulk production or repeat orders.

What We Help Define

From Fabric to Packaging, Every Detail Matters

Product development is not only about design. It is about making the garment realistic, repeatable and suitable for its market. We help clients make practical decisions before production begins.

  • Fabric composition, GSM, texture, stretch, comfort and product positioning.
  • Fit direction including regular, oversized, athletic, women's, kids or custom sizing approaches.
  • Construction details such as collars, cuffs, seams, plackets, hems, pockets and trims.
  • Decoration methods including screen printing, embroidery, sublimation or transfer printing.
  • Labels, hang tags, neck prints, packaging and retail-ready details.
  • Quote, sample and production requirements before bulk manufacturing.

Development Helps Avoid Production Mistakes

Rushing into production without clear specifications can lead to poor fit, unsuitable fabric, wrong decoration methods, inconsistent sizing or packaging problems. Product development helps identify those issues earlier, while changes are easier and less expensive to make.
