Custom Labels Complete the Product Experience

Labels are small details, but they influence how a garment is perceived. For private label brands, ecommerce stores, uniforms and merchandise, labels help the product feel intentional, organised and repeatable.

Label planning should happen before sampling or bulk production whenever possible. Placement, artwork, dimensions, material, colours and care information can affect construction, comfort and production flow.

Label Planning

Choose Label Placement Around Comfort and Brand Use

Label placement should be planned around the garment, fabric, wearer comfort and how the product will be sold or distributed. A sportswear label may need different placement from a heavyweight hoodie or corporate polo.

  • Neck labels are common for brand identity and size information.
  • Side seam labels can add branding without irritating the neck area.
  • Printed neck labels are useful for comfort-focused t-shirts and activewear.
  • Care labels should be readable and suitable for the garment type.
  • External hem or sleeve labels can support premium collection branding.
  • Label artwork should be prepared clearly before sampling where possible.

How Labels Fit Into Production

Labels are easiest to manage when they are included in the production plan early. This helps avoid late changes to seams, neck prints, care label content or packaging details.

1. Brand and Artwork Review

We review your logo, colours, artwork files and intended label type.

2. Placement Planning

We confirm where labels should sit on the garment and whether sewn or printed branding is more suitable.

3. Sample Approval

Labels can be checked during sampling so size, feel, placement and readability are confirmed.

4. Bulk Production

Approved label details are included in the production specification.

5. Packing and Organisation

Labels can support size sorting, retail presentation, branch packing and ecommerce preparation.

6. Repeat Orders

Documented label specifications help future production stay consistent.
