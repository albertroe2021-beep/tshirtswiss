From Brief to Finished Garment

A Clear Process for Professional Apparel Manufacturing

Every successful garment order starts with the right information. Before production begins, we review your product type, quantity, target market, fabric expectations, colours, sizing, branding, packaging, deadline and delivery destination. This early planning stage is important because small decisions can have a major effect on comfort, cost, durability and final appearance.

For clients with an existing tech pack, we review the specification and advise on manufacturability. For clients without a complete technical file, we help define the details needed to move forward. This may include garment measurements, fabric composition, GSM, decoration method, label placement, packaging format and carton requirements.

Because we work with corporate apparel, uniforms, custom t-shirts, private label clothing, sportswear, workwear and promotional merchandise, our process is flexible but structured. The same principles apply across every product: define the requirement, approve the sample or specification, produce carefully, check the result and ship reliably.

Production Steps

How We Manage Your Apparel Order

Consultation and Quote

We begin by understanding what you want to make, how the garment will be used and what commercial outcome you need. We then prepare a quotation based on product type, quantity, materials, decoration, packaging and shipping requirements.

Fabric and Material Planning

Fabric is central to quality. We help clients select suitable cotton, blends, performance fabrics, stretch materials, fleece, woven fabrics or technical textiles depending on the garment category and end use.

Sampling and Approval

Where needed, samples are produced to confirm fit, construction, fabric feel, colour, branding and finishing. Sampling reduces risk and gives clients a physical reference before bulk production begins.

Bulk Manufacturing

After approval, production is scheduled and managed by experienced teams. Cutting, sewing, decoration and finishing are coordinated according to the confirmed specification.

Quality Control

Quality checks are performed throughout the process. We review measurements, stitching, fabric condition, print or embroidery placement, colour consistency, trims, labels and packing.

Packing and Shipping

Finished garments are folded, packed and prepared for domestic or international delivery. We support worldwide shipping for clients in Switzerland, Europe, Australia, Asia and beyond.

What to Prepare

Information That Helps Production Move Faster

The more complete the brief, the faster we can quote, sample and plan production. Not every project needs a full technical pack, but clear information helps avoid delays and misunderstandings.

Product Details

Garment type, quantity, target wearer, intended use, fit direction, size range and reference images or samples.

Fabric Direction

Preferred fabric type, weight, stretch, performance needs, hand feel, colour and climate suitability.

Branding Details

Logo files, artwork, print or embroidery placement, labels, hang tags, packaging and brand guidelines.

Commercial Details

Target deadline, delivery destination, sales channel, packing needs and whether repeat orders are expected.

Quality Control

Quality Is Managed Before, During and After Production

Many production problems happen when quality control is left until the end. By then, mistakes can be expensive or impossible to correct. TShirt Swiss uses a more careful approach. We look at quality as a continuous process that begins with the initial specification and continues through sourcing, sampling, manufacturing, finishing and packing.

Our checks focus on the details that matter to real customers: consistent sizing, comfortable fit, clean stitching, correct logo placement, fabric suitability, colour accuracy, durable decoration and professional presentation. For private label clothing, we also review labels, hang tags, packaging and the overall retail impression. For uniforms and corporate apparel, we focus on consistency across team members and repeat orders.

Swiss-managed oversight means we communicate clearly when a specification needs attention. If a fabric is not suitable, a print method is not ideal, or a timeline is unrealistic, we prefer to explain this early rather than create problems later.

Approval Points

Where Decisions Are Confirmed

Clear approval points help protect both the client and the production team. Before bulk manufacturing, we aim to confirm the details that have the biggest effect on the final garment.

Specification Approval

Product type, measurements, fabric, colours, trims, branding method and packaging requirements.

Sample Approval

Fit, hand feel, construction, logo placement, labels, finishing and any changes required before bulk production.

Artwork Approval

Print files, embroidery files, sublimation artwork, placement dimensions and colour expectations.

Packing Approval

Folding, polybags, size stickers, carton markings, role packing, branch packing or ecommerce preparation.

Repeat Production

Building Apparel Programmes That Can Be Reordered

Many clients do not need a one-time order. They need a repeatable production system for uniforms, staff apparel, ecommerce basics, gym merchandise, event campaigns or private label collections.

Repeat production works best when specifications are documented clearly. We help record garment details, fabric direction, colour references, size ranges, decoration placement, labels, packing and carton requirements so future orders can be produced more consistently.
