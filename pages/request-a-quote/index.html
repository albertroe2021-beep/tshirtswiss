What to Include in Your Quote Request

A clear enquiry helps us understand the product, production complexity and delivery requirements. Apparel pricing depends on quantity, fabric, garment construction, decoration method, labels, packaging, sampling needs and shipping destination.

We recommend including the following details where available:

  • Garment type, such as t-shirt, polo, hoodie, uniform, rashguard, scrub, workwear, shorts, cap or tote bag.
  • Estimated quantity per style, colour and size.
  • Fabric preference, GSM, performance requirement or reference garment.
  • Logo, artwork, print, embroidery, sublimation or heat transfer details.
  • Custom labels, hang tags, packaging or private label requirements.
  • Target delivery date and shipping destination.
  • Any existing sample, tech pack, size chart or brand guideline.

Quote Accuracy

Why We Ask Detailed Questions

A useful apparel quote is not just a price per piece. It should reflect the real garment, fabric, decoration, finishing, packing and shipping requirements. A low generic price can become expensive later if important details were not included.

We ask detailed questions so the quote is realistic, comparable and aligned with the final product. This is especially important for private label clothing, sportswear, uniforms, multi-product merchandise ranges and any order that needs labels or packaging.

Details That Affect Price

  • Quantity and size range
  • Fabric type and garment complexity
  • Number of colours or artwork positions
  • Decoration method
  • Labels, hang tags and packaging
  • Sampling and approval requirements
  • Packing method and shipping destination
