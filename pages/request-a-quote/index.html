Quote Preparation

What to Include in Your Quote Request

A useful apparel quote is not just a price per piece. It should reflect the real garment, fabric, decoration, finishing, packing and shipping requirements. A low generic price can become expensive later if important details were not included.

We recommend including garment type, estimated quantity per style, colour and size, fabric preference, GSM, performance requirements, logo or artwork placement, decoration method, labels, hang tags, packaging, target delivery date and shipping destination.

For private label clothing, send the product range, target customer, label needs, packaging ideas and launch timeline. For uniforms and merchandise, send the staff group, event date, delivery location, size range and branding requirements.

The goal is to produce a realistic quote that reflects the final product, not a vague estimate that changes later.

Quote FAQ

Common Questions Before Requesting a Quote

What information do you need for a quote?

We need the garment type, quantity, artwork or logo, preferred fabric, size range, colours, delivery destination and deadline. Reference photos, tech packs, size charts and previous samples are also helpful.

Can I request a quote without a tech pack?

Yes. A tech pack is helpful but not always required. If you do not have one, send reference images, garment type, desired fit, quantity, artwork and packaging requirements.

Can you quote private label clothing?

Yes. We can quote private label projects including product development, sampling, custom labels, hang tags, packaging and repeat production planning.

Do you quote orders for Switzerland?

Yes. We work with Swiss businesses and can prepare orders for delivery to Switzerland, Europe and other international destinations.

Can I approve a sample before production?

Where appropriate, samples can be used to confirm fabric, fit, construction, artwork placement, labels and finishing before bulk production begins.

What affects apparel pricing the most?

Quantity, fabric, garment complexity, artwork positions, decoration method, labels, packaging, sampling requirements, packing method and shipping destination all affect pricing.
