Comfort and Practicality

Choosing Scrub Fabric and Fit for Daily Use

Scrubs are worn for long periods and often washed frequently. Fabric choice, seam construction, pocket position, trouser fit, stretch and breathability can affect the wearer experience every day.

We help review the role, department, climate, colour preference, washing expectations and branding method before recommending a fabric and construction direction. For dental and clinic teams, presentation may be central. For care teams, movement and easy-care performance may matter more.

  • Fabric guidance based on comfort, washing and daily movement
  • Support for scrub tops, trousers, tunics and coordinated staff ranges
  • Repeat order planning for growing teams and multi-location organisations

Embroidery and Branding

Professional Branding for Medical Scrubs

Scrub branding should feel clean, professional and durable. Embroidery is often the preferred option for clinic logos, name details and subtle uniform branding because it provides a polished finish.

Logo placement should be planned carefully around pockets, seams and daily movement. Department colour coding, tone-on-tone embroidery and simple chest logos can create a refined result without making the garment feel promotional.

Labels, size tags and packing can also support distribution across staff, departments and locations. This is particularly useful for larger teams and repeat orders.

TShirtSwiss helps align scrub fabric, garment construction and branding so the final range looks consistent and practical for daily healthcare use.

Quality Control

Checks for Fit, Stitching, Colour and Packing

Scrub quality control focuses on measurements, fit, seam strength, pocket placement, fabric consistency, colour accuracy, embroidery placement, labels and packing accuracy.

For repeat programmes, approved specifications help keep future orders aligned with previous deliveries wherever possible. This supports a consistent team appearance as staff are added or garments are replaced.
