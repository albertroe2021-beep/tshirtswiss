Fabric and Function

Choosing the Right Outerwear Fabric

Jackets and softshells should be planned around the environment where they will be worn. A corporate softshell may need a clean face fabric and subtle embroidery. A field jacket may need durability, wind resistance and practical pockets. A private label jacket may need a more refined fit, branded trims and retail-ready finishing.

Softshell fabrics are popular because they balance comfort, movement and a professional appearance. Bonded fleece can add warmth. Woven polyester can support lighter jackets and event apparel. Water-resistant and wind-resistant fabrics may be useful for outdoor staff, logistics teams and sports clubs. The best choice depends on the role, climate, budget and desired brand position.

  • Softshell, woven, bonded and lined fabric guidance
  • Support for zippers, pocket construction, cuffs, linings and trims
  • Outerwear specifications built around corporate, workwear, sport or retail use

Embroidery, Patches and Branding

Branding Methods for Custom Jackets

Embroidery is one of the most common branding methods for jackets and softshells because it gives a durable, professional and premium look. It works well for chest logos, sleeve details, staff uniforms and corporate apparel where the branding should feel polished without being too loud.

Heat transfer and specialist print methods can be suitable for larger graphics, reflective branding, event jackets or technical fabrics where embroidery may not be ideal. Woven patches, rubber badges, zipper pullers, sleeve tabs and custom labels can also create a more retail or private label appearance.

The correct branding method depends on the outer fabric, lining, decoration area, artwork complexity, quantity and intended use. Softshell and technical fabrics require careful planning because incorrect decoration can affect appearance, performance or comfort.

TShirtSwiss helps review the garment, artwork and decoration requirements before production so the final jacket looks professional and remains suitable for its intended environment.

Quality Control

Outerwear Requires Detailed Quality Checks

Jackets include more components than basic garments, so quality control must check more than logo placement. Fabric consistency, zipper quality, lining attachment, pocket construction, stitching, sleeve length, hem finishing, embroidery placement, trims and packaging all affect the final impression.

For staff jackets, consistency across sizes and repeat orders is important. For private label jackets, hang tags, labels, folding, bagging and retail finishing may be just as important as the garment construction.

  • Sample and specification approval before bulk production
  • Checks for fabric, zippers, lining, pockets, decoration and finishing
  • Packing and delivery preparation for Switzerland and international destinations
