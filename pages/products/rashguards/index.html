Performance Product

Rashguards Need More Than a Good Graphic

Rashguards are worn during high-movement training, so the garment must be planned around performance as well as appearance. Fit, stretch recovery, seam comfort, panel placement and artwork durability all influence how the product performs.

For sportswear brands, rashguards can become a hero product. For gyms and teams, they create identity. For ecommerce brands, they can anchor a complete stretch activewear collection.

Fabric Guide

Rashguard Fabric Types and Benefits

Fabric choice affects compression, stretch recovery, print result, drying speed, opacity, durability and comfort.

Polyester-Spandex

Core rashguard fabric direction for stretch, recovery and close-fitting performance wear.

Sublimation Polyester

Best for full-colour artwork, team designs, branded graphics and all-over layouts.

Compression Knits

Useful for fitted rashguards and performance-focused activewear.

Lightweight Stretch Fabric

Good for warm climates, high-sweat training and customers who prefer a lighter feel.

Heavier Stretch Fabric

Provides more structure and coverage for premium rashguards and retail brands.

Mesh or Vent Panels

Can support airflow in selected zones for hot training environments or fitness apparel.

Fit and Construction

Fit, Sleeves and Seams Matter

Rashguards can be designed as compression-fit, athletic-fit or slightly more relaxed depending on the sport and customer. Sleeve length, torso length, collar shape and seam placement all change the user experience.

Short Sleeve Rashguards

Popular in hot climates, gym use and casual sports training.

Long Sleeve Rashguards

Useful when customers want more coverage, a team-uniform look or full-sleeve artwork.

Flat or Smooth Seams

Help reduce irritation in close-fitting garments during movement.

Compression Fit

Provides a close, athletic feel for performance brands and serious training use.

Decoration

Sublimation for Full-Colour Rashguard Designs

Sublimation is one of the strongest methods for rashguards because artwork becomes part of the fabric rather than sitting heavily on top of the garment. This is useful for logos, team colours, sponsor graphics and all-over artwork.
