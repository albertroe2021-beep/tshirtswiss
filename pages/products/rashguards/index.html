Fabric and Fit

Choosing Rashguard Fabric for Stretch, Recovery and Print Clarity

Rashguard fabric needs to stretch comfortably and recover well after movement. The hand feel, weight, opacity, print response and seam construction all affect how the garment performs in training and how it looks as a branded product.

We help review the intended use, artwork, fit direction, sleeve length, size range and brand positioning before recommending a construction direction. A club training rashguard may need strong value and repeatability, while a private label product may need a more refined fit, stronger branding details and retail finishing.

  • Fabric guidance based on stretch, recovery, opacity and sublimation quality
  • Support for team graphics, sponsor logos, rank colours and private label artwork
  • Repeat order planning for clubs, gyms, teams and ecommerce brands

Sublimation and Artwork

Full-Colour Rashguard Printing and Branding

Sublimation is a strong method for rashguards because it allows full-colour artwork, team designs, sponsor logos, sleeve graphics and detailed visual identity to be integrated into the garment. It is especially useful where clubs or brands want bold artwork that is not limited to a small chest print.

Artwork needs to be planned carefully around panels, seams, sleeve placement and size grading. Sponsor logos, rank colours and academy marks should remain legible across the full size range.

For private label fightwear, branding can also include neck prints, woven labels, hang tags, barcode stickers and packaging. These details help the garment feel retail-ready rather than just printed sportswear.

TShirtSwiss helps align artwork, fit, fabric and finishing so the final rashguard looks consistent and performs in regular training use.

Quality Control

Quality Checks for Fit, Stretch, Artwork and Stitching

Rashguard quality control focuses on fabric feel, stretch, seams, print clarity, logo placement, colour consistency, measurements, labels and packing. Because the garment is worn close to the body, fit and construction are especially important.

For club orders, name, logo and sponsor details need careful review. For retail brands, labels, packaging and presentation are also part of the final product standard.

  • Sample and artwork approval before bulk production
  • Checks for fabric, fit, print, stitching, labels and packing
  • Delivery preparation for Switzerland, Europe and international destinations
