Fabric and Durability

Choosing Workwear That Holds Up

Workwear fabric should be selected for the role it needs to perform. A lightweight cotton t-shirt may work for indoor staff, while field teams may need stronger blends, performance polos, fleece layers or softshell jackets. Staff who work outdoors, travel between jobs or wash garments frequently need apparel that remains comfortable and presentable over time.

We help review the environment, washing frequency, branding method, climate, colour needs and wearer profile before recommending a fabric direction. The goal is to produce apparel that staff are willing to wear and that the business can reorder with confidence.

  • Fabric recommendations based on durability, comfort and wash requirements
  • Support for work shirts, polos, tees, hoodies, jackets and outerwear ranges
  • Repeat order planning for growing teams and long-term workwear programmes

Branding and Identification

Branding Methods for Custom Workwear

Workwear branding should be visible, durable and appropriate for the garment. Embroidery is often used on polos, jackets, caps and softshells because it has a professional, long-lasting finish. Screen printing and transfer methods can be useful for work t-shirts, larger back logos, sleeve details and job-site identification.

For teams that work in public, branding can help customers identify staff quickly. For trades and service companies, chest logos, back prints, sleeve logos and colour-coded garments can make the business look more organised and professional.

The right decoration method depends on the fabric, artwork, quantity, wash requirements and intended use. Workwear often needs more durability than promotional apparel, so we review logo placement and method before production.

Labels, packing and size grouping can also support distribution, especially when uniforms need to be issued across teams, departments or locations.

Quality Control

Workwear Quality Checks Before Dispatch

Because workwear is used repeatedly, quality control matters. Checks can include fabric consistency, garment measurements, stitching, collar shape, pocket position, print placement, embroidery quality, shade consistency, label application and packing accuracy.

For ongoing programmes, the approved specification becomes especially important. It helps keep future orders aligned with the original garment, so new staff can be issued apparel that looks consistent with the rest of the team.

  • Sample and specification approval before bulk production
  • Checks for fabric, fit, stitching, branding, labels and packing
  • Packing and delivery preparation for Switzerland and international destinations
