Sportswear Fabric Types and Benefits

Fabric selection should be based on activity, climate, stretch needs, artwork method, wash frequency and target price point. A gym merchandise shirt, compression rashguard and retail activewear top need different fabric logic.

Dry-Tech Polyester

Lightweight and quick drying for training shirts, race apparel, teamwear and warm climates.

Polyester-Spandex

Supports stretch and recovery for compression tops, activewear and rashguards.

Compression Knits

Useful for close-fitting performance garments and private label activewear.

Mesh Panels

Can improve airflow in high-heat areas of training tops and active garments.

Sublimation Polyester

Ideal for full-colour teamwear, rashguards, MMA shorts and technical artwork.

Cotton-Rich Blends

Useful for lifestyle gym apparel, fan merchandise and off-duty sportswear.

Climate and Performance

Choose Sportswear Around the Conditions

Sportswear should match the climate and training environment. A hot outdoor event, air-conditioned gym, tropical training camp and premium activewear collection can each require different fabric choices.

  • Lightweight dry-tech fabrics suit hot and humid training environments.
  • Stretch blends support movement, grappling, lifting, running and functional training.
  • Mesh details can help airflow in warm conditions.
  • Cotton-rich blends work well for lifestyle gym merchandise and fan apparel.
  • Full-colour teamwear often works best with sublimation-compatible polyester.
  • Private label sportswear should consider hand feel, packaging and repeat sizing.
