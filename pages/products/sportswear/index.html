Performance Fabric

Choosing Fabric for Movement, Comfort and Repeat Washing

Sportswear fabric needs to be planned around activity, climate and wearer expectations. Quick-dry polyester is common for training shirts and event tops. Stretch jersey supports active movement and fitted garments. Mesh panels improve breathability, while structured performance fabrics need close attention to recovery, hand feel and sizing.

We help review the intended use before recommending a fabric direction. A running shirt, club jersey, gym top, yoga set and event shirt all require different fabric behaviour and different decoration methods.

  • Fabric recommendations based on sport, fit, climate and wash requirements
  • Support for sublimation, printing, embroidery, numbers and sponsor logos
  • Team, event and private label production with repeat-order planning

Sublimation, Printing and Branding

Decoration Methods for Custom Sportswear

Sublimation is often used for sportswear because it allows full-colour designs, team graphics, sponsor logos, names and numbers to be integrated into the fabric. It is especially useful for jerseys, event shirts and garments that require all-over artwork.

Heat transfer can work well for names, numbers, sponsor logos and smaller production runs. Screen printing can be suitable for simpler designs and larger quantities. Embroidery is usually best for polos, caps, jackets or more structured sports apparel where a premium logo finish is required.

The correct decoration method depends on the fabric, artwork, quantity, sport, washing requirements and intended use. A team uniform has different needs from a private label gym collection or an event shirt.

TShirtSwiss helps align artwork, fabric and decoration so the final garment looks sharp and remains practical for active use.

Quality Control

Quality Checks for Fit, Fabric, Print and Team Details

Sportswear quality control needs to review more than appearance. Fabric weight, stretch, seam strength, measurement consistency, logo placement, number positioning, colour accuracy, label application and packing all affect the final result.

For teamwear, names, numbers and sponsor logos need careful checking. For private label sportswear, labels, hang tags, packaging and size stickers may also be part of the finished product specification.

  • Sample and specification approval before bulk production
  • Checks for fit, fabric, decoration, measurements, labels and packing
  • Delivery preparation for Switzerland, Europe and international destinations
