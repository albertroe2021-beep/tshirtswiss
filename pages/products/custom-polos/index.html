Fabric Guide

Choosing the Right Polo Shirt Fabric

Polo fabric affects appearance, breathability, wash performance, embroidery results, climate suitability and how professional the garment feels.

Cotton Pique

Classic textured polo fabric with a structured, premium look. Suitable for corporate teams, retail, golf and hospitality.

Cotton Jersey

Softer and smoother than pique, useful for relaxed polos, lifestyle uniforms and casual brand apparel.

Poly-Cotton Blends

Durable, stable and easy-care. Good for repeat uniform programmes, trades, events and frequent washing.

Dry-Tech Polyester

Lightweight, quick drying and useful for warm climates, sports clubs, outdoor staff and active teams.

Performance Pique

Combines polo structure with moisture-management benefits for golf, resorts and mobile teams.

Stretch Blends

Useful where movement and fit matter, such as hospitality, reception, wellness and premium service teams.

Climate and Environment

Match the Polo to the Workplace

A polo for an air-conditioned office does not need the same fabric as a resort uniform, outdoor event shirt or trade team polo. Selecting the right fabric improves comfort and wearability.

  • Warm and humid climates: lightweight cotton blends, dry-tech polyester or performance pique.
  • Air-conditioned offices: cotton pique, midweight blends or polos paired with light jackets.
  • Hospitality and resorts: breathable fabrics, premium trims and colour systems by role.
  • Trade and field teams: durable blends, darker colours and embroidery or print that withstands frequent use.
  • Golf and sports clubs: performance pique, stretch blends and moisture-management fabrics.

FAQ

Custom Polo Shirt Questions

What fabric is best for custom polos?

Cotton pique is the classic premium choice, poly-cotton blends are durable and easy-care, and dry-tech polyester is useful for warm climates, sports and outdoor work.

Is embroidery better than printing on polos?

Embroidery is usually best for premium logo placement on polos. Printing can work for larger artwork, event apparel or specific technical fabrics.

Can you make polos for hot climates?

Yes. Lightweight cotton blends, dry-tech polyester and performance pique fabrics are suitable for warmer or more active environments.

Can you produce private label polos?

Yes. We can add woven labels, printed neck labels, care labels, hang tags, branded buttons, packaging and repeatable specifications.

Can you support repeat uniform orders?

Yes. We can document fabric, colour, sizing, embroidery placement, packaging and specifications so future orders remain consistent.
