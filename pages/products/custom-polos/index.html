Fabric and Fit Options

Choosing the Right Polo Shirt Fabric

The fabric determines how a polo looks, feels and performs. Some clients want a classic cotton pique polo with a structured look. Others need lightweight polyester for warm climates, dry-tech fabrics for active teams or blended fabrics that balance softness, durability and easy care.

We help clients choose polo fabrics based on comfort, washability, climate, brand positioning and end use. A luxury hospitality polo may need a different fabric from a trade uniform or a sports club polo. The best fabric is not always the most expensive option; it is the one that performs correctly for your purpose.

Cotton Pique Polos

A classic option with texture, breathability and a traditional corporate appearance. Suitable for premium uniforms, retail and hospitality.

Cotton Blend Polos

Blended fabrics can improve durability, reduce shrinkage and offer a more stable fit for repeat uniform programmes.

Performance Polos

Polyester, dry-tech and moisture-management fabrics are useful for sports teams, outdoor staff and warm working environments.

Custom Fit Development

Options can include regular fit, slim fit, women's fit, relaxed fit, long sleeve polos and custom size charts for brand-specific requirements.

Decoration and Branding

Embroidery, Printing, Labels and Premium Details

Embroidery is one of the most popular branding methods for polo shirts because it gives a professional, durable and premium finish. It works especially well for chest logos, sleeve logos, hospitality uniforms, corporate apparel and branded team clothing. We can also advise on print options where artwork, fabric or budget makes printing more suitable.

Branding decisions should consider logo size, stitch density, fabric weight, placement and garment use. A heavy embroidery on a lightweight polo may not sit correctly, while a subtle tone-on-tone logo can look premium for hospitality or executive apparel. Our team reviews these details before production so the finished garment looks balanced.

For private label and retail polo shirts, we can add custom woven labels, printed neck labels, care labels, hang tags, size stickers, folded packaging and branded polybags. These details help transform the product from a generic polo into a complete brand experience.
