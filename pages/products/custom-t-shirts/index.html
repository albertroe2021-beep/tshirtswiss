Fabric Guide

Custom T-Shirt Fabric Types and Benefits

Fabric choice affects hand feel, breathability, shrinkage, durability, print result, price, climate suitability and customer perception.

100% Cotton

Classic, breathable and familiar. Good for everyday t-shirts, band shirts, creator merch and retail basics.

Combed Cotton

Smoother and more refined than basic cotton. Strong option for premium private label t-shirts.

Heavyweight Cotton

Creates a structured streetwear feel for oversized tees, premium drops and higher-value merchandise.

Poly-Cotton and CVC Blends

Reliable, soft and practical for uniforms, events, staff shirts and repeat programmes.

Performance Polyester

Quick-drying and useful for sports, fitness events, tropical climates and active teams.

Stretch Blends

Add movement for fitted styles, activewear-inspired shirts and garments where flexibility matters.

Organic or Recycled Options

Suitable when sustainability is part of the brand story or procurement requirement.

Dry-Tech Fabrics

Designed for moisture management, warm-weather use and sportswear applications.

GSM and Weight

Choosing the Right T-Shirt Weight

GSM affects how the t-shirt feels, drapes, prints, ships and sells. A lightweight promotional shirt is not the same as a premium streetwear tee or private label basic.

  • Lightweight: useful for warm climates, events, sports, giveaways and budget-sensitive orders.
  • Midweight: balanced choice for corporate teams, merchandise, ecommerce basics and staff apparel.
  • Heavyweight: best for premium retail, band merch, creator drops, oversized fits and streetwear-style products.
  • Performance weights: chosen around movement, breathability and drying speed rather than cotton-like structure.
Printing and Decoration

Screen Printing, Embroidery, Sublimation and Transfers

Different t-shirt designs require different decoration methods. We select the method around artwork, fabric, quantity, garment use and brand positioning.

Screen Printing

Excellent for band shirts, merch drops, corporate t-shirts, events and bold artwork at scale.

Embroidery

Premium for small logos and subtle branding, especially on heavier t-shirts, polos, caps and layering pieces.

Heat Transfer

Useful for names, numbers, sponsor logos, smaller runs and certain technical fabrics.

Sublimation

Best for polyester performance shirts and full-colour all-over artwork.

Printed Neck Labels

Useful for private label t-shirts where the product should feel fully brand-owned.

Woven Labels and Hang Tags

Add retail value for ecommerce brands, creator merch and premium product drops.

Climate and Workplace Suitability

Choose the T-Shirt Around the Environment

Climate and working conditions matter. A hot outdoor event, air-conditioned office, tropical gym, concert venue and ecommerce streetwear drop each need different fabric and fit decisions.

Hot and Humid Conditions

Lightweight cotton, cotton blends or dry-tech polyester can improve comfort and drying speed.

Premium Retail

Combed cotton, heavyweight cotton and clean finishing support higher perceived value.

Active Use

Performance polyester, stretch blends and breathable constructions support movement and sweat management.

Frequent Washing

Poly-cotton blends, stable construction and durable decoration help staff shirts and uniforms last longer.

FAQ

Custom T-Shirt Manufacturing Questions

What fabric is best for custom t-shirts?

It depends on the use case. Cotton is classic and breathable, combed cotton feels more premium, heavyweight cotton suits streetwear and merch, while polyester or dry-tech fabrics are better for sports and warm climates.

What GSM should I choose?

Lightweight fabrics suit events and warm climates, midweight fabrics work for most corporate and merch use cases, and heavyweight fabrics are best for premium retail, creator drops and streetwear-style products.

Can you make private label t-shirts?

Yes. We support printed neck labels, woven labels, hang tags, care labels, size stickers, packaging and repeatable production specifications.

Is screen printing or embroidery better?

Screen printing is usually better for larger artwork and t-shirt graphics. Embroidery is better for smaller premium logos, especially on heavier garments or structured apparel.

Can you produce band and creator merchandise?

Yes. We manufacture t-shirts for bands, creators, ecommerce stores, fan communities, tours, events and limited product drops.
