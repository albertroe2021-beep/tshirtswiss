Campaign Planning

Choosing Merchandise That People Will Actually Use

Promotional merchandise should do more than display a logo. Useful, well-made products are more likely to be kept, worn, shared and photographed. That means the product choice, fabric, print quality, packaging and overall presentation should be aligned with the audience and campaign goal.

We help review the purpose of the merchandise, event date, audience, budget, brand guidelines, product mix and delivery location before recommending a production approach. A corporate conference may need clean, practical products, while a lifestyle brand may need more retail-ready finishing.

Branding Methods

Printing, Embroidery and Finishing for Promotional Merchandise

Different products require different branding methods. Screen printing is well suited to t-shirts, tote bags and larger graphic applications. Embroidery works well for caps, polos, jackets and premium staff merchandise. Heat transfer and sublimation can support more detailed artwork in selected use cases.

For promotional campaigns, consistency across products matters. A cap, tote bag and t-shirt should feel like they belong to the same campaign even if each item uses a different decoration method.

Private label or retail-style merchandise can also include woven labels, printed labels, hang tags, barcode stickers, branded bags and custom packaging.

TShirtSwiss helps align the product mix, artwork, decoration method and finishing so your merchandise programme feels cohesive and professionally managed.

Quality Control

Quality Checks Across Multiple Product Types

Promotional merchandise quality control focuses on product consistency, print clarity, embroidery placement, label application, size grouping, colour alignment, packing accuracy and delivery preparation.

When a campaign includes multiple products, the packing plan becomes especially important. Event kits, retail bundles and multi-location deliveries need clear grouping and carton planning so the final delivery is easy to distribute.

  • Sample and artwork approval before bulk production
  • Checks for product quality, branding, labels, packing and quantities
  • Delivery preparation for Switzerland, Europe and international destinations
