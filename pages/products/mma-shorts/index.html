Fabric and Fit

Choosing MMA Shorts for Mobility and Durability

MMA shorts need to support kicking, grappling, sprawls and repeated high-intensity movement. Fabric weight, stretch, waistband security, leg opening, side slit design and seam placement all affect comfort and performance.

We help review the intended use, artwork, fit direction, size range and brand positioning before recommending a construction direction. A gym short may need durability and repeatability, while a retail fightwear short may need refined finishing, labels and packaging.

  • Fabric guidance based on movement, comfort, print method and durability
  • Support for club graphics, sponsor logos, all-over artwork and private label details
  • Repeat order planning for gyms, promotions, teams and ecommerce brands

Sublimation and Branding

Full-Colour MMA Shorts Printing and Private Label Details

Sublimation is a strong method for MMA shorts because it supports all-over artwork, sponsor logos, gym branding and vivid fightwear designs. It also allows designs to be integrated into the garment instead of relying only on small logo placements.

Artwork should be planned around seams, panels, side slits, waistband placement and size grading. Logos and sponsor marks need to remain readable across the full size range.

For private label fightwear, finishing can include woven labels, printed labels, hang tags, barcode stickers, custom packaging and retail folding.

TShirtSwiss helps align artwork, fabric, cut and finishing so the final MMA shorts feel cohesive and practical for training or retail sale.

Quality Control

Quality Checks for Fit, Artwork, Stitching and Waistband

MMA shorts quality control focuses on measurements, waistband security, stretch panels, stitching, leg opening, print clarity, colour consistency, label application and packing accuracy.

For fight teams and promotions, sponsor logos and club marks need careful review. For ecommerce brands, labels, packaging and presentation are part of the final product standard.
