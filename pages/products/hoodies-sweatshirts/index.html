Product Strategy

Hoodies Are High-Value Apparel Products

For many brands, hoodies and sweatshirts are not giveaway items. They are premium merchandise, retail products, staff layers or community apparel. That means buyers should consider fabric quality, garment weight, decoration method and packaging before production begins.

A hoodie for a band merch table has different priorities from a corporate team hoodie, a gym warm-up layer or a streetwear ecommerce drop. We help clients select the right garment direction rather than using one generic hoodie for every project.

Fabric Guide

Hoodie and Sweatshirt Fabric Types

Fabric choice affects warmth, drape, shrinkage, print results, embroidery stability, perceived value and climate suitability.

Brushed Fleece

Soft inner surface and warmer feel. Good for premium hoodies, winter merch, team apparel and higher-value products.

French Terry

Loop-back construction that feels lighter and more breathable than heavy fleece. Useful for warmer climates, lifestyle sweatshirts and layering.

Heavyweight Cotton Blends

Strong streetwear feel with premium structure. Suitable for oversized hoodies, limited drops and ecommerce brands.

Poly-Cotton Fleece

Durable and stable for corporate apparel, uniforms, events and repeat programmes.

Performance Fleece

Useful for gyms, sports teams and travel apparel where lighter weight and quicker drying are useful.

Organic or Recycled Options

Suitable when sustainability is part of the brand story or buyer requirement.

GSM and Weight

Choosing the Right Hoodie Weight

Hoodie weight influences warmth, cost, shipping weight, perceived value and climate suitability. A lightweight sweatshirt may be better for tropical climates, while a heavy hoodie may be better for premium merch or cold-weather apparel.

Lightweight

Good for warmer climates, travel, layering and casual sweatshirts where breathability matters.

Midweight

Balanced choice for corporate teams, gyms, events and general merchandise.

Heavyweight

Best for premium streetwear, band merch, limited drops and high-value ecommerce products.

Oversized Fits

Popular for streetwear and creator brands where silhouette is part of the product value.
