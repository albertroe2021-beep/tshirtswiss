Product Strategy

Muay Thai shorts can become the centre of a gym uniform, athlete collection or private label product range. For international brands, Thailand production provides a strong story, while Swiss-managed communication helps keep specifications, timelines and quality expectations clear.

Fabric Guide

Muay Thai Shorts Fabric Types and Benefits

Fabric choice affects shine, movement, weight, artwork method, drying speed and retail feel. Traditional-looking shorts and modern performance shorts may need different fabric directions.

Satin Polyester

Classic shiny look often associated with Muay Thai shorts. Good for bold colour, movement and traditional styling.

Gloss Polyester

Creates a bright visual finish for teams, events and product drops that need strong shelf or photo impact.

Sublimation Polyester

Useful for all-over artwork, gradients, sponsor layouts and detailed graphics.

Lightweight Performance Fabric

Good for hot climates, active training and customers who prefer a lighter short.

Polyester Blends

Durable, stable and practical for team ranges, gym merchandise and repeat production.

Contrast Trims

Waistband, piping and panel details can strengthen team colours and brand identity.

Cut and Construction

Waistband, Side Slits and Fit Matter

Muay Thai shorts need to allow movement while maintaining a recognisable silhouette. The waistband, leg opening, side slit height, length and panel shape all influence comfort and appearance.

Traditional Cut

Shorter, wider silhouette with strong visual identity and classic sport styling.

Modern Training Cut

Can be adjusted for brands wanting a slightly cleaner or more contemporary look.

Elastic Waistband

Important for fit, comfort, stretch and garment stability during movement.

Side Slits

Support leg movement and help define the traditional Muay Thai shorts shape.
